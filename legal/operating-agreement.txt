OPERATING AGREEMENT
OF
TITRA HEALTH LLC
A Georgia Limited Liability Company

Effective Date: April 9, 2026

============================================================

ARTICLE I — FORMATION AND NAME

1.1  Name. The name of the limited liability company is Titra Health LLC
     (the "Company").

1.2  Formation. The Company was formed under the Georgia Limited Liability
     Company Act (O.C.G.A. § 14-11-100 et seq.) (the "Act") by filing
     Articles of Organization with the Georgia Secretary of State on
     April 9, 2026.

1.3  Principal Office. The principal office of the Company shall be at an
     address determined by the Members from time to time.

1.4  Registered Agent. The registered agent and registered office shall be
     as set forth in the Articles of Organization, as amended from time
     to time.

1.5  Purpose. The Company is formed for the purpose of developing,
     marketing, and operating the TitraHealth mobile application and
     related health technology products, and any other lawful business
     the Members may determine.

1.6  Term. The Company shall continue in existence until dissolved in
     accordance with this Agreement or the Act.

============================================================

ARTICLE II — MEMBERS AND MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

2.1  Members. The Members of the Company are:

     Name                        Membership Interest
     --------------------------  -------------------
     Ibrahim Mohammad                   50%
     Ray Wade                           50%

2.2  Capital Contributions. Each Member's initial capital contribution,
     if any, is set forth in Exhibit A attached hereto.

2.3  No Additional Contributions. No Member shall be required to make
     additional capital contributions without the unanimous written

2.4  No Interest on Capital. No Member shall receive interest on any

2.5  Return of Capital. No Member has the right to demand the return of

ARTICLE III — MANAGEMENT AND VOTING

3.1  Member-Managed. The Company shall be member-managed. All Members
     shall have the right to participate in the management of the
     Company, subject to the voting provisions below.

3.2  Voting Power. Voting power shall be allocated as follows,
     independent of membership interest percentages:

     Name                        Voting Power
     --------------------------  ------------
     Ibrahim Mohammad                  51%
     Ray Wade                          49%

3.3  Ordinary Decisions. All ordinary business decisions of the Company
     shall be decided by a simple majority vote of the Members based on
     their Voting Power. Because Ibrahim Mohammad holds 51% of the
     Voting Power, he shall have tie-breaking authority on ordinary
     business matters.

3.4  Major Decisions Requiring Unanimous Consent. Notwithstanding
     Section 3.3, the following actions shall require the unanimous
     written consent of all Members:

     (a) Sale, merger, or dissolution of the Company;
     (b) Admission of new Members;
     (c) Amendment of this Operating Agreement;
     (d) Incurring debt or obligations exceeding $10,000;
     (e) Sale or encumbrance of substantially all Company assets;
     (f) Changing the purpose or nature of the Company's business;
     (g) Issuance of additional membership interests or equity;
     (h) Entering into any contract or commitment exceeding $25,000
         in value;
     (i) Filing for bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings;
     (j) Changing the Company's tax classification.

3.5  Managing Member. Ibrahim Mohammad shall serve as the Managing
     Member responsible for the day-to-day operations of the Company,
     including but not limited to:

     (a) Managing the Company's bank accounts;
     (b) Executing contracts in the ordinary course of business;
     (c) Making hiring and operational decisions;
     (d) Representing the Company in dealings with third parties.

3.6  Duties of Members. Each Member shall devote such time and effort
     to the Company's business as is reasonably necessary for the
     Company to achieve its objectives.

============================================================

ARTICLE IV — ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

4.1  Profits and Losses. The net profits and net losses of the Company
     shall be allocated among the Members in proportion to their
     Membership Interests (50% / 50%).

4.2  Distributions. Distributions of available cash shall be made at
     such times and in such amounts as determined by the Members,
     allocated in proportion to their Membership Interests (50% / 50%).

4.3  Tax Distributions. The Company shall make reasonable efforts to
     distribute to each Member, at least quarterly, an amount sufficient
     to cover such Member's estimated tax liability arising from the
     Company's income allocated to such Member.

4.4  Limitation on Distributions. No distribution shall be made if,
     after giving effect to the distribution, the Company would not be
     able to pay its debts as they become due in the ordinary course of
     business.

============================================================

ARTICLE V — ACCOUNTING, RECORDS, AND TAX MATTERS

5.1  Fiscal Year. The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar
     year.

5.2  Books and Records. The Company shall maintain complete and accurate
     books and records of account. All Members shall have reasonable
     access to inspect and copy Company records.

5.3  Bank Accounts. The Company shall maintain one or more bank accounts
     in the Company's name. All Company funds shall be deposited in such
     accounts and shall not be commingled with personal funds of any
     Member.

5.4  Tax Classification. The Company shall be classified as a
     partnership for federal income tax purposes unless the Members
     unanimously agree to elect otherwise.

5.5  Tax Matters Partner. Ibrahim Mohammad shall serve as the "Tax
     Matters Partner" (or "Partnership Representative" under the
     Bipartisan Budget Act) and shall be responsible for all tax filings
     and communications with tax authorities on behalf of the Company.

============================================================

ARTICLE VI — TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTERESTS

6.1  Restrictions on Transfer. No Member may sell, assign, transfer,
     pledge, or otherwise dispose of all or any portion of their
     Membership Interest without the prior written consent of all other
     Members, except as otherwise provided herein.

6.2  Right of First Refusal. If a Member receives a bona fide offer
     from a third party to purchase all or any portion of such Member's
     Membership Interest, the selling Member shall first offer to sell
     such interest to the remaining Member(s) on the same terms and
     conditions. The remaining Member(s) shall have thirty (30) days
     to accept or decline the offer.

6.3  Permitted Transfers. A Member may transfer their interest to a
     trust, estate planning vehicle, or wholly-owned entity for the
     benefit of the transferring Member without triggering the Right
     of First Refusal, provided the transferring Member retains
     control of the transferee entity.

============================================================

ARTICLE VII — WITHDRAWAL, DEATH, AND DISSOLUTION

7.1  Voluntary Withdrawal. A Member may withdraw from the Company upon
     ninety (90) days' written notice to all other Members. The
     withdrawing Member's interest shall be purchased by the remaining
     Member(s) at fair market value as determined by mutual agreement or,
     if the Members cannot agree, by an independent appraiser.

7.2  Death or Incapacity. Upon the death or permanent incapacity of a
     Member, the deceased or incapacitated Member's Membership Interest
     shall pass to their estate or legal representative. The remaining
     Member(s) shall have the option (but not the obligation) to
     purchase such interest at fair market value within one hundred
     twenty (120) days.

7.3  Dissolution. The Company shall be dissolved upon:
     (a) The unanimous written consent of all Members;
     (b) The sale of all or substantially all Company assets;
     (c) A judicial decree of dissolution; or
     (d) Any event that makes it unlawful for the Company to continue.

7.4  Winding Up. Upon dissolution, the Managing Member shall wind up
     the Company's affairs, liquidate its assets, and distribute
     proceeds in the following order:
     (a) Payment of debts and liabilities to creditors;
     (b) Payment of any outstanding tax distributions;
     (c) Distribution of remaining assets to Members in proportion to
         their Membership Interests.

============================================================

ARTICLE VIII — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

8.1  Company IP. All intellectual property created by any Member in
     connection with the Company's business, including but not limited
     to the TitraHealth mobile application, source code, designs,
     trademarks, and trade secrets, shall be the sole property of the
     Company.

8.2  Assignment. Each Member hereby assigns to the Company all right,
     title, and interest in any intellectual property created in the
     course of Company business.

8.3  Post-Departure. Upon a Member's withdrawal or removal, they shall
     have no continuing rights to the Company's intellectual property.

============================================================

ARTICLE IX — INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

9.1  Limited Liability. No Member shall be personally liable for any
     debt, obligation, or liability of the Company solely by reason of
     being a Member.

9.2  Indemnification. The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless each
     Member from and against any claims, losses, or liabilities arising
     from actions taken in good faith on behalf of the Company, except
     for acts of willful misconduct, gross negligence, or fraud.

============================================================

ARTICLE X — NON-COMPETE AND CONFIDENTIALITY

10.1 Non-Compete. During their membership and for a period of twelve
     (12) months following withdrawal or removal, no Member shall
     directly or indirectly engage in any business that competes with
     the Company's GLP-1 health tracking and titration management
     business within the United States, without the written consent of
     the other Member(s).

10.2 Confidentiality. Each Member shall keep confidential all non-public
     information concerning the Company's business, including user data,
     financial information, trade secrets, and proprietary technology,
     both during and after their membership.

============================================================

ARTICLE XI — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

11.1 Mediation. Any dispute arising under this Agreement shall first be
     submitted to mediation by a mutually agreed-upon mediator.

11.2 Arbitration. If mediation fails to resolve the dispute within
     thirty (30) days, the dispute shall be submitted to binding
     arbitration in the State of Georgia under the rules of the
     American Arbitration Association.

11.3 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed
     in accordance with the laws of the State of Georgia.

============================================================

ARTICLE XII — GENERAL PROVISIONS

12.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement
     among the Members with respect to the Company and supersedes all
     prior agreements and understandings.

12.2 Amendments. This Agreement may be amended only by the unanimous
     written consent of all Members.

12.3 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found to be
     invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue
     in full force and effect.

12.4 Notices. All notices required under this Agreement shall be in
     writing and delivered to the Members at their addresses on file
     with the Company.

12.5 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each
     of which shall be deemed an original.
